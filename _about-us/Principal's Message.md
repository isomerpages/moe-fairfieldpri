<p>Dear Parents and Guardians,</p>
<p>Term 2 has been a season of remarkable achievement, joyful discovery,
and courageous endeavour at Fairfield. Our 2026 theme "Strong and Courageous"
has truly come alive as we witnessed our students rise to meet challenges
with determination, embrace new experiences with boldness, and celebrate
one another's successes with genuine community spirit.</p>
<p>We are immensely proud of our students' outstanding performances across
different areas this term. Our Chinese Dance, Choir, and Concert Band each
brought home the Certificate of Distinction at the Singapore Youth Festival,
a testament to dedicated practice and the courage to perform at their very
best. Our Malay Language students shone brightly, with one team clinching
Third Place at Bisikan Pena and others making their mark representing Fairfield
for the very first time at the Piala Bangsawan drama competition. One of
our STEM Club teams also demonstrated exceptional critical thinking by
breaking into the top 15 teams nationally at the 2026 National Thinkers
Challenge. Beyond these highlights, many of our students have worked very
hard and competed with tenacity in the National School Games across our
sports CCAs, and others challenged themselves intellectually at competitions
such as the Mathematics and Science Olympiads, and these are but a few
of the many moments of courage and excellence our students have shown this
term. These achievements, spanning the arts, languages, sports, and innovation,
remind us that strength and courage take many forms, and we celebrate each
one of them.</p>
<p>Beyond competitions, Term 2 was filled with enriching experiences that
brought learning to life in joyful ways. For example, students delighted
in Chinese stories in the Media Resource Library and explored creativity
through the Makers Programme during recess. The English Department's Book
Character Day was a particular highlight, with teachers and students dressing
up as their favourite literary characters, filling our school with colour,
laughter, and a shared love of reading.</p>
<p>We are deeply grateful for the safe and meaningful learning journeys our
students embarked on beyond our school gates. From the Primary 3 Supermarket
Shopping Trips, Primary 5 Outdoor Adventure Camp at Sentosa to the international
trips to Shandong, China and Hong Kong, we give thanks to God for watching
over each of our students and keeping them safe throughout these adventures.</p>
<p>I would also like to express my sincere appreciation to our parent community
for your steadfast partnership. The smooth and successful running of the
Mid- Year Meet the Parents session and the Parent-Teacher Conference would
not have been possible without your enthusiastic participation and support.
Beyond these sessions, we are equally grateful to our ever-supportive Partners
in Education (PiE), who have generously given of their time and energy,
volunteering countless hours to support the running of our many school
programmes this term. Together, your dedication and involvement are a powerful
reminder to our students that their growth and well-being are cherished
by the entire Fairfield community, and we are deeply grateful for your
continued partnership.</p>
<p>As we look ahead to Term 3, we have much to celebrate together. We will
be marking Racial Harmony Day and National Day with joy and pride, and
we especially look forward to celebrating our 138th Founder's Day. As part
of this meaningful occasion, we will once again be organising Sophia Blackmore
Oaks (Our Acts of Kindness), this year in partnership with the Care Community
Service Society (CCSS). We invite our Fairfield community to donate daily
necessities to be assembled into care packs for our support staff, children
and the elderly from CCSS, while our staff will also be making donations
to support ex-offenders. In doing so, we honour the legacy of our founder,
Miss Sophia Blackmore, whose compassion and selfless service continue to
inspire every Fairsian. We look forward to the active participation of
every parent and student, as this is a heartfelt expression of our Fairsian
Outcome of Serving God and the Community.</p>
<p>May our students carry forward the confidence and courage they have built
this term, face new challenges with resilience, and continue to uplift
one another. I extend my heartfelt gratitude to our dedicated teachers,
whose tireless commitment and love for our students make Fairfield a place
where every child is empowered to flourish.</p>
<p><strong><em>"For the Spirit God gave us does not make us timid, but gives us power, love and self-discipline.” ~ 2 Timothy 1:7</em></strong>
</p>
<p>Serving with you,
<br><strong>Mrs Soh Mei Foong</strong>
<br>Principal</p>
